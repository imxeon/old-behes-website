By Hook or by Crook:
  1. In 1947, 75% of what is now Indian territory was made up of over 560 princely states. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel took up the task of uniting them under Indian statehood using the 'Instrument of Accession'. Research the instrument of accession, it's tricky history and its implications on Indian national identity.
    2.
  2. All political systems have their quirks and strange traditions. Some of the things you might see as 'normal' in your own country might be outlandish everywhere else. Explore the long- standing tradition of joke candidates at democratic elections. Discuss with your team: Does voting for a joke or fictional candidate serve as a protest against the current candidates? Or is it little more than a symbol of voters' frustration?
    3.
  3. The idea that governments often lie to their citizens is not a new one. In fact, conspiracy theorists have even alluded to the fact that the japanese attack on Pearl harbor and 9/11 were false flag operations. Read about the Gleiwitz incident and discuss with your team:Do you think the strategy the nazis used was effective? Would you feel comfortable knowing your government adopted similar strategies? And finally, should governments be able to lie to their citizens?
    4.
Brewing in the Boardroom : Corporate Games
  1. Explore the Cola Wars between two of the world's biggest corporations - Pepsi and Coca-Cola. Discuss with your team : Do corporate rivalries help the consumers or hinder them? Can you think of any more corporate rivalries from the current world? What impact have they had on consumer's lives?
    2.
  2. Read about the chess matches between the then Chess world champion Garry Kasparov and IBM's Deep Blue. Do you see this as a battle between man and machine? Did IBM cheat? Was Kasparov just a sore loser? What are the lessons here about the future of Artificial Intelligence?
    3.
  3. That companies spy on each other in order to gain an advantage is common knowledge, but we see such activities as being the realm of 'serious' industries. Research the 'chocolate wars', a long period of intense competition (and skullduggery) between leading chocolate manufacturers.
    4.
